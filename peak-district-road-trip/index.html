The trip

England's first National Park — and its cheapest big adventure

The Peak District sits perfectly between the North and Midlands, so six of you can pile into one car, split the fuel, and spend a weekend on gritstone edges, in stone villages and beside mirror-still reservoirs. Nearly everything worth seeing — the hills, the dales, the views — is completely free. This guide keeps the wallet closed and the boot full of snacks.

Best places to visit

The must-do spots

A mix of easy wins and proper adventures — all car-accessible with parking nearby.

Iconic ridge walk

Mam Tor & the Great Ridge

The “Shivering Mountain”. A paved path to a 517m summit with 360° views over Edale and the Hope Valley. Walk the ridge to Lose Hill if legs allow.

Free entryFree roadside parking nearby
Village + castle

Castleton & Peveril Castle

Postcard stone village, home base for caves (Blue John, Speedwell), cafés and pubs. Norman castle ruins above with cracking valley views.

Village freeCastle/caves ticketed
Reservoir + Dambusters

Ladybower & Derwent Dam

Vast reservoir with flat, easy trails (great for a big group). Derwent Dam was the Dambusters WWII practice site. Hire bikes at Fairholmes.

Free to roamCheap car park
Gritstone edge

Stanage Edge

4-mile gritstone escarpment beloved by climbers and Keira Knightley (Pride & Prejudice). Sunset here is unreal. Easy access from Hook's Car.

Free entryCheap parking
Stepping stones

Dovedale

The famous stepping stones across the River Dove and a gentle riverside walk up to Thorpe Cloud. Gets busy — arrive early with the crew.

Free entryCheap NT car park
Market town

Bakewell & Monsal Head

Home of the Bakewell pudding, riverside strolls and a Monday market. Drive to Monsal Head for the classic viaduct viewpoint photo.

Town freeCheap car parks

Save the money

Free & cheap parking

Of the ~45 National Park car parks, 13 are free. Here are the best free spots plus the cheap paid ones worth it for prime locations.

🟢 Free parking

Mam Tor roadside (Old Mam Tor Rd) — laybys past Treak Cliff Cavern; short walk to the ridge.

Hurst Clough, Upper Derwent — ~15 spaces beside Ladybower.

Upperdale (Monsal Trail) — ~10 spaces near Monsal Dale.

A6013 laybys — near Ladybower for reservoir walks.

Surrounding villages — free residential streets (park considerately) in Edale & Hathersage outskirts.

🟡 Cheap & worth it

Fairholmes (Ladybower) — the hub for reservoir + Dambusters; reasonable NP rates.

Bakewell Agricultural Centre — big, central, cheaper than town-centre bays.

Hook's Car (Stanage) — closest to the edge; a few £ for the day.

Dovedale NT car park — for the stepping stones; NT members free.

💡 Tip: with 6 sharing one car, even paid parking is pennies each — but the free spots keep the whole trip near-zero.

Fuel the crew

Cheap eats & proper pubs

Big portions, small prices. These are the local legends that won't wreck the group budget.

Budget legend

Grindleford Station Café

Cult cheap-eats institution by the railway tunnel. Enormous fry-ups, chip butties and the famous “no chips with everything” signs. Cash-friendly, walker-approved.

££ · huge portions
Castleton

Three Roofs Café & The George

Three Roofs does cream teas & sarnies at the village entrance; The George fills Derbyshire oatcakes with local bacon. Bulls Head for a pint after.

£–££ · café + pub
Bakewell

Bakewell Pudding Shop

You can't leave without the original Bakewell pudding. Monday street food market mixes hog roast with Thai noodles — cheap and cheerful.

£ · sweet treat
Edale

The Old Nag's Head & Bowling Green

Start/finish of the Pennine Way. Hearty pub grub — slow-cooked lamb, pies and moorland ale — perfect after Mam Tor.

££ · pub food
Deals

The Maynard / The George, Hathersage

Midweek deals: 50% off steaks (Wed) & Burger + Drink £15 (Fri) at The Maynard; 2-for-1 pizza at sister pub The George.

££ · deal days
Self-cater

Picnic + Co-op runs

Biggest saver: grab a Co-op/Aldi shop in Bakewell or Hathersage, pack sandwiches and brew flasks. A reservoir picnic beats any restaurant markup.

£ · cheapest option

Group hacks

6-in-a-car survival kit

🅿️ Arrive before 10am

Popular car parks (Castleton, Dovedale) fill fast on weekends. Early = free roadside spots and no queues.

💷 One kitty

Everyone drops £40 into a shared pot for fuel, parking & snacks. Splitwise the rest. Fewer awkward maths at the pub.

👟 Real shoes

Trails get muddy and rocky. Trainers survive the easy walks; boots for Mam Tor/Stanage in wet weather.

📶 Download maps offline

Signal drops in the dales. Save this map's spots to Google Maps offline before you leave the M1.

🥪 Picnic = king saver

A Co-op shop + reservoir picnic feeds 6 for the price of one pub main.

🌦️ Check the forecast

Mountain weather turns fast. Pack layers & a waterproof even in summer — and flip the itinerary for sun.
